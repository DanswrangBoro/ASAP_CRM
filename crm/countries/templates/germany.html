Discover the Beauty of Germany

The Federal Republic of Germany is the official name of the country located in western Central Europe. Germany is bounded to the north by the North and Baltic Seas and to the south by the Alps. The word Germany derives from the Latin Germania, which came into use after Julius Caesar adopted it for the peoples east of the Rhine. Germany as a whole was organized into two separate politics with limited sovereignty: The Federal Republic of Germany, generally known as West Germany, and the German Democratic Republic, known as East Germany, while Berlin continued its de jure four-power status. There are five terrestrial ecoregions that make up Germany's territory: The Atlantic mixed forests, the Baltic mixed forests, the Central European mixed forests, the Western European broadleaf forests, and the mixed and coniferous Alps forests. There are sixteen Lander, or constituent states, that make up the federation of Germany. It has a network of 227 diplomatic missions abroad and maintains relations with more than 190 countries. Germany is well known for such folk festival traditions as the Oktoberfest and Christmas customs.

German cuisine varies from region to region, and often neighbouring regions share some culinary similarities, including with the southern regions of Bavaria and Swabia, Switzerland, and Austria. Also, Germany is one of the leading motor sports countries in the world. And also for the most popular sport, football. The standard of living in Western Germany is among the highest globally. Germany has long been significant to Western culture as the home of key philosophical and artistic movements, as well as the modern printing press. of wealth compares favourably with that of other advanced countries. The visual arts have a long and illustrious history in Germany. German artists were first exposed to the three-dimensionality of Roman art during the reign of Charlemagne in the Middle Ages.German architecture has incorporated elements of other European styles with its own distinctive national identity throughout its history.
